¿WHY FILTER THE COFFEE?

Article 1

Concept 1: As we have already seen, currently the coffee industry in Mexico and in the world is committed to
improving processes, ensuring traceability and correct handling of the seed until it is roasted, all
this path requires that the methods to extract its aromatic and flavor compounds will adapt and evolve
so that they offer a better perspective, perception and experience to the final consumer.

Article 2

The main difference with the infusion or immersion methods is that in these the total water remains in
contact with the coffee particles for as long as is decided until the drink is strained by some means
and served. On the other hand, in filtering methods, the water passes through the coffee bed at a
certain speed that allows it to take with it the greatest amount of compounds, sediments, and solubles
from each coffee particle. This flow must be constant and balanced. , that is to say, it must have an
equal flow rate in each part of the coffee bed, this is achieved with a granulometry according to the
method and depending on the time it is expected to take to "wash" the coffee, another one also enters
variable such as turbulence, we will talk about these variables in more detail

¿WHAT VARIABLES INFLUENCE THE FILTERING?

Water temperature.

The temperature favors the volatilization of compounds, as well as the separation of these to be
transported to the final drink, the temperature in SCA parameters should be between 92° and 96° although
in recent times this range has been opened since It has been seen that temperatures above 65-70° favor
the extraction of some compounds, depending on the grain .

Turbulence.

This variable refers to the movement of the water, this is achieved by adding the water, in spiral or
similar movements, the force of this pouring and/or even with the use of a spoon or "shovel" (as in the
case of the AeroPress ). The turbulence helps to increase the soluble action of the water, that is, it
increases the speed of extraction of the compounds from the coffee particles. This depends to a large
extent on the time that is decided for the final extraction, since strong turbulence in prolonged times
can result in over-extractions and bitter or unpleasant flavors, and on the contrary, little turbulence
and in a short time will give us under-extraction. extractions and flat flavors or with little
permanence in the mouth.

Extraction time.

It is one of the most important variables since the final result in the cup will depend on the time it
takes for the water to extract the compounds, short times could give flat flavors, and prolonged
extractions can result in strong, bitter or undesirable flavors. For the filtering methods, the times
range from 2:00 to 5:00, depending on the method.

Grinding or granulometry.

This variable is used to choose the "recipe" for each extraction, that is, the amount of coffee, water
and the time needed, a coarse grind will require longer times, as well as a fine grind, short times. As
we have already seen in previous deliveries.

  1. Choose the right grinder: There are two main types of coffee grinders: blade grinders and burr
    grinders. Burr grinders are considered to be superior since they produce more consistent grounds,
    but they tend to be more expensive.
  2. Choose the grind size: The grind size you choose depends on the brewing method you will use. Coarser
    grinds are used for methods like French press, while finer grinds are used for espresso.
  3. Measure the coffee: Measure out the amount of coffee you want to grind. Use a kitchen scale if
    possible for accuracy.
  4. Grind the coffee: Add the coffee beans to the grinder and grind them according to the desired grind
    size. Pulse the grinder in short bursts to prevent overheating the coffee.
  5. Check the consistency: Check the grounds to make sure they are consistent in size. You may need to
    shake or tap the grinder to ensure all the beans are ground evenly.
  6. Store the grounds: Once the coffee is ground, store it in an airtight container to prevent moisture
    and air from degrading the flavor.

Extraction ratio.

As we already saw in espresso, the ratio refers to the amount of water per coffee, for extractions ideal
ratios of 1:12 to 1:18 are considered, it all depends on the amount that we are going to prepare and
what we want to extract , short ratios give us intense extractions, and long ratios, flatter cups, like
a barista, over time ratios between 1:13.5 - 1:16.5 have had very good results, of course depending on
the coffee and the method, however this We will only know this data based on the experimentation and
taste of the barista or coffee enthusiast.

Method and filter.

¿What is?

In general, the filtering methods are cones or semi-cones in which paper filters are placed (although
there are also metal, cloth or glass filters), the angle of the cone, as well as the shape of the walls
(as in the V60 whose name is taken from the 60° angle of the cone, and that on its inner face it
presents spiral grooves that favor the extraction flow), as well as the quality of the filter paper and
its shape directly influence the flow of water and the amount of solubles and sediments that pass or not
towards the final drink. It is important to know the method we use and the quality of its filters in
order to better choose the methodology to follow.

Chemex.

The Chemex method is known for producing a clean, bright cup of coffee with a lighter body and a crisp
finish. The thicker filter removes more oils and sediment from the coffee, resulting in a clearer and
more delicate flavor profile. The Chemex is a popular choice among coffee enthusiasts and is often used
in specialty coffee shops and homes for manual brewing.

Dripper V60

The V60 method is known for producing a clean, crisp cup of coffee with a bright and nuanced flavor
profile. The cone shape and ridges of the dripper promote a more even extraction of the coffee, while
the paper filter removes some of the oils and sediment, resulting in a clearer and more delicate taste.

AeroPress

The Aeropress method is known for producing a clean, smooth, and rich cup of coffee with a low acidity
and minimal bitterness. The plunger creates a vacuum that helps to extract the coffee evenly, while the
paper filter removes sediment and oils, resulting in a clear and consistent flavor profile.

French press

The French press method is known for producing a full-bodied, rich, and flavorful cup of coffee. The
metal or nylon mesh filter allows for more oils and flavors to pass through into the final cup,
resulting in a coffee with a full and complex flavor profile. However, the French press can sometimes
produce coffee with sediment or grounds in the cup, which some people may find unappealing.
